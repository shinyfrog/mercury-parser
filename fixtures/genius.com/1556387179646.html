About “Angel”

Released as a single from the 1997 album Surfacing, the song “Angel” started out as a totem to a string of deaths in the music industry in relation to drug overdoses and suicides. The main death which had convinced Sarah Mclachlan’s decision to write a song was the death of Smashing Pumpkin’s touring pianist, Jonathan Melvoin, who died from a heroin overdose at the age of 34.

This death wasn’t the first nor the last to bring shock to the musical industry, but due to how well known and loved Jonathan Melvoin was, three artists (one being Sarah Mchlachlan) wrote songs directly narrating the problems within the music industry.

Ever since the song’s initial debut, it has seen several covers, sample uses, as well as it being used in commercials for the ASPCA.
